ACM MMSports 2022

5th International ACM Workshop on Multimedia Content Analysis in Sports

Challenges

This year, MMSports proposes a competition where participants will compete over State-of-the-art problems applied to real-world sport specific data. The competition is made of 4 individual challenges, each of which is sponsored by SportRadar with a $1’000.00 prize.

Each challenge comes with a toolkit describing the task, the dataset and metrics on which participants will be evaluated:

  • Ball 3D localization
  • Camera calibration
  • Instance segmentation
  • Players re-identification

The competition will run in two phases. For the first phase, participants compete to have their method published in the conference proceedings. For the second phase, participants compete for a $500,00 prize. Participant are allowed (and encoraged) to participate in both phases. In addition, for each challenge, a jury prize of $500,00 will be awarded to team proposing the most innovative solution, regardless of the performance on the leaderboard.

Submissions

The challenges are hosted on EvalAI where participants will submit the prediction of their model on an evaluation set for which labels are kept secret. In addition, each submission should be acompagnied with a technical report of at least 3 pages describing the proposed approach and a the code to reproduce the results. Reports are to be uploaded to ArXiv and the code to a public repository. The associated links must be provided within the EvalAI submission.
